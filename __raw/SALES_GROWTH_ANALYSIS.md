## **Q&A:**

### **Q: Why did Southwest accelerate while others decelerated?**

**A:** Southwest's unique position:
1. **Fuel hedging:** Locked in lower fuel prices pre-crisis
2. **Domestic focus:** Avoided international travel collapse
3. **No legacy costs:** No pension obligations weighing down competitors
4. **Market share gains:** Passengers traded down from legacy carriers
5. **Strong balance sheet:** Could invest while others cut back

Result: Crisis became opportunity (+3.3pts acceleration)

### **Q: Why did Singapore collapse so badly?**

**A:** Perfect storm of vulnerabilities:
1. **Premium positioning:** Business/first class demand evaporated
2. **Long-haul international:** Routes hit hardest by crisis
3. **Asian exposure:** Asian markets crashed first (Sept 2008)
4. **High cost structure:** Couldn't compete on price when demand shifted
5. **Limited domestic market:** Singapore too small for domestic buffer

Result: 10.2% growth â†’ 0.1% (worst crisis impact)

- Natural market expansion

## **Technical Notes:**

### **Why separate 2007 and 2008 charts?**

**CFA's concern:** Combined charts don't clearly show which year

**Solution:**
- Two separate bar charts (2007, 2008)
- Same Y-axis scale (0-25%) for visual comparison
- Different colors (Blue = pre-crisis, Orange = crisis)
- Each clearly labeled with year in title

**Benefit:**
- Immediate visual comparison side-by-side
- Shows trend (who accelerated vs decelerated)
- Avoids confusion about which data is which year

### **Alternative: Side-by-side clustered column?**

**Pros:**
- Shows both years in one chart
- Direct comparison

**Cons:**
- Cluttered (12 bars total)
- Hard to read exact values
- Loses individual year focus

**Recommendation:** Use separate charts for clarity, add third "change" chart for trend analysis.
